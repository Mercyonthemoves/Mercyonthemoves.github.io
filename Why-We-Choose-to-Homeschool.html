Top 4 Reasons Why We Choose to Homeschool

Mercyonthemove

1

Home ·Homeschool ·Top 4 Reasons Why We Choose to Homeschool

As someone who tends to over analyze it was overwhelming for me trying to decide whether or quite some time before we took the leap. He was onboard from the start and in fact it was his idea. I balked at this initially as I had quite a few concerns, most of which was tied to my presumed inability to be an effective parent and teacher. As time went on though and the conversations evolved and of course our lives changed things began to come into focus and we decided to give it a chance after narrowing down our reason to these top four that I will be sharing with you today. To be frank, deciding to homeschool has been one of the best decisions we could have made for our family.

Academic

This was one of the major reasons we chose to homeschool our children. You may have a child that is struggling academically in a traditional school setting and needs a bit more attention in this department. Homeschool offers the opportunity for parents and care givers alike to take control and work individually with their children on each subject to mastery at their own pace.

The same goes for a child that perhaps is exceeding their academic expectations, in which case the child is free to work ahead at their own pace. To put it plainly, homeschooling offers the freedom to tailor the curriculums to one’s individual needs.

Flexibility

The flexibility that comes with homeschooling has been immeasurable. You have so much freedom in terms of your time and the location in which you choose to school. Homeschooling offers more time as a family because you can plan your schedule to allow for more quality time and by that, I mean that a typical school day lasts approximately 3 to 4 hours for us and the rest of our time we spend doing the things we enjoy the most. There is also the flexibility that it allows in terms of learning through exploration.

By this I mean most of our daily activities can be turned into an avenue of learning for instance dinner time can be math time by teaching measurements and time keeping skills. Learning can be done when inspiration strikes and as such no hard and fast rules on scheduling because let’s face it…we don’t always feel like doing things at the same time every single day. Sometimes we learn in the morning and sometimes we choose the evenings. At times Saturdays and Sundays work best. In short there are no hard and fast rules; you do what works best for your family.

Independence

Homeschool encourages an environment that fosters independence in children and also allows for the freedom of individual expressions. I believe that as parents we all want for our children to feel free to be themselves; to think outside the box and be okay with themselves. Homeschooling offers this opportunity and as parents it is such a joy to watch them grow and learn about who they really are outside of who society tells them that they are and have to be.

Safe Learning Environment

To homeschool is synonymous with ensuring that child is learning in a safe environment. We have all seen and heard stories of what is taking place in some schools all over the world and if we could do anything to help minimize these dangers when it comes to our children, we would no questions asked.

Overall, deciding whether or not to homeschool is a personal decision and one that must be made taking into account the emotional demand that it does require and the individual needs of all parties involved and one that should be made while addressing those needs.
