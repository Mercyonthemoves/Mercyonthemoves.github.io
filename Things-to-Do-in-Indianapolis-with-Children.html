Things to Do in Indianapolis with Children Over the Weekend

1

Home ·Travel ·Things to Do in Indianapolis with Children Over the Weekend

Whether you are looking for an adventurous getaway or a laid-back weekend in the heart of the city, Indianapolis has got you covered. Indianapolis is the capital and most populous city in the entire state and as such has got a lot in terms of diversity, entertainment and activities to keep you and your little ones entertained for hours. I have compiled a list of my family and Is’ top seven activities that we engaged inn the last time we took the city out for a spin and without further ado let’s begin!

  1. The Children’s Museum of Indianapolis

    2. This museum is a family favorite and contains lots of activities to keep children of all ages entertained for hours. This museum has got it all when it comes to having a great balance of educational and entertaining exhibits. Children can have hands on experience with their learning and trust me they will not want to leave.

    3000 N Meridian St, Indianapolis, IN 46208
    (317) 334-4000

  2. Monument Circle

    3. There is so much to do and see at this zoo. It is just large enough to kill enough hours all the while keeping children and adults alike entertained. Tons of biodiversity to explore here and of course if possible do check out the Christmas at the Zoo fest around the holiday season.

    1200 W Washington St, Indianapolis, IN 46222
    (317) 630-2001

  3. Indianapolis Zoo

    4. Located at the heart of downtown monument circle showcases the statues of soldiers and sailors of years past. It is a sight to behold and a beautiful spot for some iconic family pictures. Especially, against the water fountains. 1 Monument Circle Indianapolis, IN 46204
    (317) 232-7615

  4. Horse Drawn Carriage ride

    5. Once you are done exploring Monument Circle and the magnificent structures around it then you and the family can grab a horse drawn carriage ride from the area and see the circle and parts of downtown through a different vantage point. Make sure you take a picture or two with your new horse friend!

  5. Donuts at Longs’ Bakery

    6. While in Indy you have to check out Longs’ Bakery! Try their fresh baked warm donuts…they are divine.

    2301 E Southport Rd, Indianapolis, IN 46227
    (317) 783-1442

  6. Speedway Indoor Karting

    7. If you have the need for speed then make sure you and the family head on over to the Speedway Indoor Karting and explore the various obstacles and tracks including the multi-level courses in the arena. This Indoor course is great for all ages and always a hit!

    1067 N Main St, Speedway, IN 46224
    (317) 870-3780

  7. Atomic Duckpin Bowling

    8. This bowling alley is a vibe! It is a vintage inspired alley with eight lanes. This alley has got a few billiards table and a café all located in the Fountain Square Theatre Building. I highly recommend for a fun filled game night.

    1105 Prospect St, Indianapolis, IN 46203
    (317) 685-1955

    Places to stay

    If you are in needed of possible places to stay in the city, I have a couple of suggestion listed below. Our family favorite is the Embassy Suites as we enjoy their breakfast offerings quite a bit but both hotels are great options in terms of amenities. With which ever one you choose do enjoy your stay and adventures in the heart of Indy!

  8. JW Marriot Downtown Indianapolis

    9. S West St, Indianapolis, IN 46204
    (317) 860-5800

  9. Embassy Suites Downtown Indianapolis

    10. 110 W Washington St, Indianapolis, IN 46204
    (317) 236-1800
